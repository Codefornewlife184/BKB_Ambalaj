    Compostable


    The raw material of compostable bags comes from sources such as cornstarch and sugarcane.

    Besides these, some are produced from microorganisms, algae, or fungi.

    Compostable bags are biodegradable.

    They are bags that can completely decompose in nature.

    The reason they are called biodegradable bags is because the decomposition process begins when they are disposed of in nature.

    Although it is an organic material, it has a very long shelf life.

    The decomposition process of compostable bags begins when suitable conditions are met after being disposed of in nature.

    At the end of the decomposition process, it mixes with the soil as fertilizable soil (humus). Additionally, it can be recycled.
